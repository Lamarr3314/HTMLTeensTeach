Overrated

Lamarr Olive

  In an attempt to analyze the public opinion of a topic combined with the actual function, humans haven’t yet come up with a straightforward formula. Instead, we tend to use the term, underrated and overrated, distinguishing something with a negative or lackluster public percephen with great value from something with and overwhelming public perception with underperforming function. When discussing whether something is overrated, there are typically three separate groups of individuals that use the term: those who have disappointed expectations, those who hate the discussed topic, and the bandwagon followers. All of these opinions combine to impact the status of a topic, whether it is overrated or not. But is there any legitimacy to this status?
 The beginning of the overrated web is the glorification of a position or subject. For instance, if an individual greatly values popularity and what it would be like to be famous, this is where something being overrated begins. As a result of this glorification, that individual works relentlessly to become famous, perfecting their craft until they have accomplished their goal. But in this transition to becoming famous, there is no sudden switch in environment or demeanor, but rather a gradual evolution of an individual and their public perception. Looking back to their original goal of fame, that individual realizes that they are pretty much the same as they were when they set out to achieve their dreams. Gradually, the reality of fame and popularity begins to fall short of their perceptions. From there, that individual believes that fame is overrated, simply due to the fact that they are still a similar individual, abiding by the same laws, in the same body. This is something that happens to many famous people across the world. For instance, when Billie Eillish set out with the goal of being a famous musician, she honed her craft, sacrificed her childhood, and eventually reached her goals. However, she often mentions how being famous is not exactly as she expected it to be, as she gave up her childhood for her dreams. However, the success of Billie Eillish begins to get envie from onlookers, who now want to do what she does. This in turns, restarts the cycle of something being overrated.
 However, this failed expectations of these individuals are not the only contributor to the perception of something being overrated. Much of this believe of something being overrated is based on the perception of the individual. This individual further pushes the idea that something is overrated, simply based on their beliefs. Going back to the Billie Eillish example, some individuals may dislike her music. This individual will see the success of Billie Eillish, listen to her music, and they just won’t like it. In their mind, things won’t add up, based on the popularity of Eillish, she must make amazing music. In reality, popularity and function are not linear. There is no exact equation that states that the more popular someone is, the better their music will be. As a result, when the magnitude of an individual’s popularity does not match up with their performance, something just doesn’t make sense. As a result, a narrative begins to form. Now, Billie Eillish is overrated. Slowly, that narrative begins to spread, expanding the overrated web. Throughout all of history, there tends to be an anti thesis as well as a thesis. This thesis will be well established and solidified throughout a long time span. However, gradually, there begins to be an anti thesis, led by contrasting minds, who believe in an alternative, the anti thesis. This same pattern occurs within social life. Some truly believe in the anti thesis, whereas others just want to be contrarian; just want to be different from the rest. When there is a large instance of popularity occurring within a product or individual, that is the thesis. However, as occurs within history, there is an opposition to this thesis. This opposition is the overrated narrative. Individuals see the widespread success of something and simply want to express an opposing idea. They desire to be different, just for the sake of being different. This contrarian narrative begins to spread, further spreading the overrated web.
 The terms underrated and overrated, are utilized to establish a correlation between public perception of a subject or and individual and their actual value. However, this concept of something being overrated is largely arbitrary and presents three separate groups of individuals: those who have disappointed expectations, those who hate the discussed topic, and the bandwagon followers. These individual combine to create the overrated web, a dynamic cycle in which public perception is altered and as a result a narrative is formed.
